Summary (2-pass reproducible signals)

  • Retention that drives tomorrow open: progress recaps (weekly/monthly), forgiving streaks, right-time nudges. Apps without them see quiet subscription death in weeks.
  • Streak failure = "disappointed parent" churn. Missed days + shame UI kills return. Clean restart without failure proof wins users back.
  • Internal attachment > external triggers: 62.8% D1 retention zero-push when "Dino is waiting" (universal morning habit made game-like and owned). Attachment creates pull.
  • First-glimpse stickiness: low-friction capture (dictation) that yields immediate personal intention for next day. Users look forward when it delivers agency fast.
  • Ownership/local data as trust accelerant: repeated "want ownership not permission"; local-first keeps data on device; full lifecycle (collect → govern/delete/export) is the eval lens for second brains.
  • Concrete attachment framing outperforms grand/abstract (soul/core/muse) in early habit formation.
