1. Does 'mirror / blind-spot / 360-for-self-understanding / Johari' land better than 'peer evaluation/rating'?

Yes — significantly better.

Consumer tools that survived and are actively used frame exactly as self-awareness mirror, not evaluation.

  • johari-window.com: "Discover how you see yourself — and how others see you". "Spot what’s shared, hidden, and new to deepen self-awareness." "Compare perspectives with the people who know you best." Shareable link + adjective selection (positive + nohari negative option). Testimonials focus on "amazed at how positively people see me" and team-building value. https://johari-window.com/
  • SelfStir Individual: Explicit "friends" or "family" survey context. "A safe and anonymous environment." "intended for development, pure and simple." "highlight ... blind spots." "increased self-awareness ... how you come across to people." 30k+ users, 100k+ raters claimed. Privacy and confidentiality heavily marketed. https://www.selfstir.com/individual-solution/
Contrast — "peer evaluation / rating" framing risk
Peeple ("Yelp for people", real-name rating of friends/exes/colleagues) triggered immediate mass backlash: privacy panic, harassment fears, "people are not products", death threats to founder. Site taken down fast. Positivity pivot failed to recover. WIRED · Business Insider

2. Positive+strengths-only at launch, then optionally balanced — appetite + risks

Appetite for positive/strengths-first: high for entry.
tbh (2017) and Gas (2022) used pre-written positive-only compliment polls ("most likely to...", "gassing up"). Both hit #1 App Store in teen segments via school targeting + FOMO invites. Constrained positive reduced toxicity vector. Wikipedia tbh · Gas · Business Insider

Live Johari tools default to or prominently feature positive adjective lists; users report "helpful" and "positive" experiences in controlled/team settings. SelfStir allows competency selection but markets strengths + blind spots discovery.

Risks of positive-only (and why phased is smart)
  • Politeness / sugarcoating bias: raters (especially friends) default to nice. Blind spot discovery diluted. Prior peer research noted "friends are 예쁘게 말하거나 속마음 숨길 거다".
  • Perceived as fluffy or low-value: "not real feedback" criticism appears in LinkedIn endorsement discussions and some 360 commentary.
  • Short-term virality, long-term churn: tbh acquired by FB then shut for "low usage"; Gas acquired by Discord then steep decline and shutdown 2023. Positive constraint helped initial safety/virality but not retention at scale. The Information (Gas shutdown)

Balanced rollout appetite: Lower at start, higher post-trust among growth-oriented users. Workplace data shows balanced 360 still carries 35%+ stress/strain even with anonymity and process. In consumer personal circle: trust is everything. Phased approach (positive launch → optional "growth suggestions" mode after 1-2 successful rounds + education) aligns with caution in psychsafety.com: "use with care... awareness can feel uncomfortable... without context risks undermining trust". psychsafety.com Johari caution

PositivePsychology.com notes potential for "discomfort or even psychological distress" if feedback received without proper framing/support. PositivePsychology Johari

Recommendation: Launch strengths-primary / positive reflections + one "helpful observation for growth" (structured). Make balanced (more direct constructive) an explicit later opt-in with strong disclaimers, aggregate views first, easy hide/delete, and "this is for your model only" education. Test with small pilot circle.

3. Optimal invite-message copy — maximizes safe adoption, reduces judgment-anxiety

Core anxiety drivers from signals: fear of negative verdict, relationship damage, "what if they think less of me", lack of control, public exposure, sugarcoating suspicion, obligation pressure.

Reducing mechanisms that work (from live tools + best practice + backlash patterns):

  • Explicit "optional / no pressure"
  • "Trusted / close circle / people who know you best"
  • "For my self-understanding / personal growth / mirror exercise" (not "so you can tell me the truth about me")
  • "Results private to me only" + "you decide what to share"
  • Strengths / positive / "helpful observation" language first
  • Low time commitment (2 min, structured link)
  • Recipient control emphasized (even if rater side)

Recommended primary invite copy (short message + link)

"Hey [Name], I'm doing a small self-understanding exercise (Johari-style mirror) with a few people I trust. Goal is to see how I come across vs how I see myself — blind spots and strengths. Would you be open to sharing 1-2 things you think are my strengths or one observation that might help me grow? Completely optional, takes ~2 minutes via link, everything stays private to me. No pressure at all — only reply if it feels good."

Why it works: growth framing + optional + private + trusted + strengths-first + low friction + explicit permission. Matches johari-window.com and SelfStir safe language. Avoids Peeple/NGL/Sarahah triggers.

Variant for more structured / strengths-primary (good for first round)

"I'm running a quick personal growth check-in with my closest circle. Using a simple mirror tool (like Johari). If you're up for it: pick a couple strengths you see in me that I might underplay, or one way I could show up better. Link is private, only I see results, and it's optional. Takes a minute. Grateful either way."

In-app / email subject + body short version

Subject: "Quick mirror exercise — optional (2 min)"
Body: "I'm collecting outside perspectives on myself for a self-model accuracy thing. Only from people I trust. Positive reflections or one growth note welcome. Private to me. Link below if you want to contribute — zero obligation."

What to avoid in copy:

  • "Rate me", "evaluate", "score", "judge"
  • "Be honest / tell me what you really think / no filter"
  • "My weaknesses" or "what I'm bad at"
  • Implied obligation ("help me out", "I need this")
  • Any hint of public, leaderboard, or mutual reveal without explicit consent
  • God-mode / reveal paid features (NGL backlash precedent)

Live evidence: johari-window.com uses neutral "invite them to describe you" + shareable link. SelfStir emphasizes safe/private/development. Professional 360 request templates stress "growth and development", "no career impact", "confidential". Personal consumer version must be stricter on warmth + optionality.

Overall recs for 2nd-B (framing + rollout)

  1. Positioning language lock: "Outside view (밖에서 본 나)", "mirror", "blind spot as growth cue", "self vs others perspective for accuracy", "trusted circle insights". Never "peer rating", "eval", "feedback score". Aligns with projects/2nd-B/social-peer-eval-architecture.html existing "내가 본 나 / 밖에서 본 나" split and "성장 단서".
  2. Launch positive/strengths-primary with structured prompts (adjective list or "strength + one observable helpful note"). Recipient-only visibility by default. Explicit education: "This is one data point for your model, not verdict."
  3. Invite flow: opt-in, small circle (3-8), mutual connection first, no contact scraping. Copy variants above + A/B test "growth" vs "understanding" phrasing.
  4. Phased balanced: gate behind completed positive round + user toggle. Always aggregate or hidden-by-default for sensitive items. Strong recipient controls (hide, delete, pause feature).
  5. Guardrails from history: no open free-text anon, no public anything, toxicity/moderation hard, easy exit, clear "this feature is optional and core journal works without it".
This framing validation reduces the creepiness/judgment vector identified in prior peer-feedback research while preserving the scientific value of informant perspectives for self-model accuracy.
