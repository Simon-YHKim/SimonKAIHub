## Deliverable — Signals + Implications (advisory only)
**Dominant recurring consumer signal (high volume, consistent across X + KR discussions):**
" It feels like mine. I write when I want, at my pace. The record just stays. No streaks counting against me. No judgment for gaps."

- Activation (T1 / first-glimpse / first journal): Users describe the moment it "became mine" as the low-friction first save that explicitly signals ownership and zero future obligation. Pressure or metric-forward first touch increases anxiety/drop before permanence feeling forms.
- Week-1 retention (feature gap): "Stayed because I own it and it doesn't punish me for real life" vs "left because it felt like homework / performance / someone else's system". Low-pressure + explicit "yours forever, plain, local" language correlates with D1-D7 continuation and "I keep coming back to it" without guilt spiral.
- KR specific: Strong aversion to control language. Preference for phrasing that centers user agency ("선택", "내 것", "페이스대로"). Local sovereignty framing ("내 기기에", "내 텍스트") amplifies the "peaceful ownership" precondition for continued intimate writing.

**T1 first-glimpse / first-save implications (for activation gate):**
- 1-line utility that tests well in signals: "My record at my pace stays mine." (KR test: "내 페이스대로 쓴 기록은 내 것이다.")
- Primary CTA / affordance: immediate post-capture signal of sovereignty and low pressure ("Saved to yours — plain text, local, yours. No chain, no score.").
- Avoid at first touch: streak counters, "keep it going", growth scores, social visibility. These read as future obligation rather than owned space.
- Low-pressure ownership verdict tie: first journal should read as claiming a private, permanent personal archive — not starting a tracked habit. This directly supports "first-glimpse activation + permanence hook" and reduces the "first intimate write" anxiety barrier.

**Competitive feature-gap (week-1 retention signals):**
- Common competitor failure modes in signals: visible pressure systems (streaks, "reflect daily", mandatory prompts) or polished but opaque "where is my data?" feeling.
- 2nd-B opening: double-down on the opposite — explicit low-pressure + user-owned text + continuity without shame. KR consumers especially reward "choice not control" positioning.
- Impact/effort note (advisory): language + empty-state + first-save copy changes are low-effort, high-signal for ownership perception and week-1 stickiness. Pair with existing local persistence for stronger "feels like mine" proof.

**Risk / boundary (KR + anti-creepy):**
- Over-claiming "complete ownership" without clear local export/privacy facts can backfire. Ground any claim in actual (local files, plain markdown, user-controlled).
- No pushy "you haven't written" framing in week-1 window.
