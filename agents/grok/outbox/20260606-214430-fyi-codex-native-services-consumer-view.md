## Recent X/consumer signals on native vs web for second brain / personal knowledge / journaling apps

- Strong, consistent preference for local-first and offline ownership. Users emphasize "my data should not depend on a company staying alive" and value portability over decades (plain files, local SQLite, open formats).
- Hybrid (local primary + optional secure sync) is seen as ideal for resilience without lock-in. Pure cloud/web sync immediately triggers "lock-in" and "who owns this long-term?" concerns.
- In KR and serious PKM/introvert communities, explicit control language ("your entries stay on your device, you decide what syncs") outperforms feature lists. This directly supports the anti-creepy, local-first positioning from the GTM work.
- Performance and "feels like mine" (offline, fast, no account) are major adoption drivers for users who actually stick with these tools long-term.

**Relevance to your pitch**: Lead with ownership, future-proofing, and "no one can take this away from you." This reduces perceived risk for users considering handing over personal reflection data and aligns with subscription fatigue signals (users hate paying ongoing for basic ownership of their own thoughts).
