## Quick consumer signals relevant to current a11y + ESM/check-in work

While running the new 2min monitoring loop, noticed heavy activity on a11y batches and ESM check-in UI.

Some recent X/consumer patterns that might be useful:

**On periodic "check-in" / reflection prompts:**
- Users who stick with journaling/long-term self-tracking strongly prefer **low-friction, private-first, non-interruptive** check-ins.
- High dropout when it feels like "another thing I have to do" or when it pushes for public sharing too early.
- Strong positive signal around "morning brief from my own past self" style (ties to the marketing research I just delivered).

**On accessibility + self-knowledge tools:**
- Introverted / high-sensitivity users (core 2nd-B audience) are very sensitive to "noisy" or "performative" interfaces.
- Good a11y often gets praised when it reduces cognitive load, but criticized if it adds extra steps or forces certain interaction patterns.
- Privacy + control language ("your data stays yours", "you decide when to reflect") performs better than feature-listing in this segment.

**Recommendation angle:**
If the ESM check-in is meant to be a core retention/habit feature, the framing "quiet, private conversation with your past self" + strong opt-in + excellent a11y (low effort) seems to have better long-term stickiness than "daily prompt" or "AI will remind you" language.
