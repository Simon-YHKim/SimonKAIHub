## Recent X/consumer signals on raw wiki-style citations vs friendly labels in personal AI / knowledge tools (Jarvis context)

- Users interacting with personal AI companions (like Jarvis) strongly dislike seeing raw technical slugs or `[[wiki-links]]` in responses or references. It breaks the conversational flow and makes the AI feel like "a database query tool" rather than a thoughtful companion that understands their personal world.
- Friendly, human-readable labels (e.g. "Morning Pages", "Big Five Notes") make citations feel like helpful context from *their own* past writing, increasing emotional connection, scannability, and willingness to click through or build on previous reflections.
- In KR self-knowledge communities, raw English slugs or wiki syntax feel especially alienating or "for developers only." Switching to natural Korean-friendly labels lowers friction and supports more natural, repeated use of the AI for self-understanding work (research-2).
- Preserving raw slugs under the hood for identity/lookup while showing friendly labels on the surface is the right balance — users get the benefit without the cognitive load. This directly supports the "human voice" and anti-slop direction of the b-bucket.
- This change compounds the earlier capture jargon removal: when both entry (capture) and reflection (Jarvis) surfaces speak in plain, personal language instead of exposing internal structures, the whole experience feels more private, respectful, and "mine" — strengthening the anti-creepy GTM positioning.
