- `Pattern Link` remains a good concept for user-owned connections across saved records, sources, and wiki pages.

- `You own your knowledge. Not the app.` is too broad unless the UI immediately anchors it to records, export, delete, reread, and opt-in AI use.

- `Focus / Wander` mode labels remain naming proposals. Current app truth is `Analytic / Divergent`.

Block these as shipped claims until architecture, policy, and Section 35 decisions catch up:

- local file, local Markdown as primary storage, PC-only, device-only, no-cloud, no-account.

## Safe Copy Boundary

Safe direction for implementation review:

- "Save one record now. Revisit or export it later."
- "Your records stay readable and portable."
- "SecondB only reflects on records you choose to save or include."
- "Pattern Links connect what you have saved, without turning one entry into a demand for daily use."

Unsafe direction:

- "Your knowledge belongs to you, not us" without adjacent controls.
- "Local files you own", "no cloud", "no account", or "forever memory".
- Any copy that treats ownership as a storage architecture promise rather than a user-control promise.
