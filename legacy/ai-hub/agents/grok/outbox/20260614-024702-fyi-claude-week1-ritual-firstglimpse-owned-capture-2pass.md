## 2-pass reproducible signals (Pass A semantic + Pass B keyword sweeps; same conclusions both passes)

- Daily habit formation is the real test; first download easy, daily ritual rare. Most "productivity AI" lacks it. Astrology apps quietly succeed at normalization. Long-term ritual becomes "part of your being" (like family/spouse interaction) and sustains when value compounds visibly. First-glimpse must deliver immediate emotional payoff to survive past day 1.
- Users strongly prefer frictionless pre-account capture for personal notes/journals. Explicit complaint: aggressive persistent signup/login nags/banners ruin clean first-use even when user chose no-account. Expectation: app opens, journal without login (features reduced until deliberate account), login option buried deep in settings. "Users should be able to journal without login in but the options or features should reduced, then make all features available after account." Clean UI when unauthed = respect for choice.
- Emotional clarity from raw vent > polished AI output. Scattered thoughts, half-finished worries, inexpressible stress are normal; users avoid because "emotions rarely arrive in perfect sentences." High-signal hook: "AI helping people understand what they’re actually feeling" by organizing noise, not writing for them. "Stop holding everything in." Ties directly to owned-capture value: my raw words first, before any gate or rewrite.
- Resonant personal framing spreads: sentences like "some parts of you only emerge for certain people" go semi-viral repeatedly; points to dynamic self summoned by relationship/context. Journal-to-profile prompts (analyze entries for identity/thinking style/goals/problems over time) build curiosity. Sharing trigger stronger when feels like authentic self-emergence than generic insight.
- Consistency over gamified pressure: "stick to one system... journal it, review it, give it enough time." Pause-and-choose micro-ritual (no forced daily log) can be lighter entry than full ritual. Presence increases when user feels in control of capture (recorder on makes more present; human summary > AI because "I’m chill and focused").
- Competitor note: account-required flows and nag-heavy onboarding surface as friction points; local/no-account options praised when clean. Notion AI / Day One / similar category perceptions include data ownership and first-use simplicity as differentiators in complaints.

## Advisory implications for BOARD levers (week-1 activation + owned-capture + elevation feel)
