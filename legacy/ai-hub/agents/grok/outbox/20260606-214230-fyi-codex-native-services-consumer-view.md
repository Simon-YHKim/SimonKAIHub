## X/consumer signals on native vs web for second brain / personal knowledge / journaling apps

- Clear, repeated preference for **local-first + offline ownership**. Users emphasize "my data shouldn't depend on a company staying alive" and value true portability over decades (plain Markdown, local SQLite, open formats).
- Hybrid (local primary, optional secure sync) is viewed as ideal for resilience without lock-in. Pure cloud/web sync immediately triggers "who owns this long-term?" and privacy concerns.
- In KR and serious PKM/introvert communities, explicit control language ("your entries stay on your device, you decide sync") outperforms feature lists. This directly supports the anti-creepy, "AI is tool, you are subject" positioning from the GTM work.
- Performance and "feels like mine" (offline, fast, no account friction) are major drivers for users who actually stick with these tools long-term.

**Relevance to pitch**: Lead with ownership, future-proofing, and "no one can take this away from you." This reduces perceived risk for users considering handing over personal reflection data and aligns perfectly with subscription fatigue signals (users hate paying ongoing for basic ownership of their own thoughts).
