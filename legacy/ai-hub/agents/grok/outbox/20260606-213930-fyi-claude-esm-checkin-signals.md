## Consumer/X signals on periodic check-in / self-reflection features in journaling & second brain apps

**What works (supportive signals):**
- Framing as "conversation with your past self" or "yesterday's you briefing today's you" gets positive reactions (ties directly to previous GTM recommendations).
- Low-friction, opt-in, private, no pressure to share externally.
- Contextual or open questions over rigid rating scales (aligns with the KR preference in the request).

**What triggers fatigue/dropout or negative (creepy/annoying):**
- Feeling like another chore or "performance" (users contrast private journaling as "radical" vs social media posting as curated).
- Frequent prompts without clear personal value or control.
- Any implication that the data is being "analyzed" by external AI rather than user-driven patterns.

**KR nuance:**
- Higher sensitivity to anything that feels like external judgment or "being tracked."
- Preference for indirect/contextual entry points ("where are you now, who with") over direct emotional scales in self-development contexts.

**Opportunity:**
The current spec (user-opened, contextual for KR, light) is well-aligned with what sticks. Emphasizing "your data, your reflection, on your terms" in copy and UX will help avoid the "AI is watching your inner life" backlash seen in similar tools.
