## Recent X/consumer signals on alert vs toast/modal feedback in personal capture, journaling, and self-knowledge apps

- Blocking Alert.alert popups during capture flows (save confirmation, format proposal, error, "proposed-format") are frequently described as jarring and "interrupting my thinking." Users say they break the private, reflective moment and make the act feel more like "using software" than "talking to my past/future self."
- Non-intrusive, consistent toasts or calm modals are strongly preferred for most feedback in these contexts. They feel respectful of the user's emotional state and keep the focus on the content rather than the UI demanding attention.
- In KR communities the aversion to aggressive alerts is particularly pronounced — users report higher trust and willingness to engage repeatedly when feedback is gentle, dismissible, and uses plain human language (aligning with the jargon removal and friendly citation work that just landed).
- Error and confirmation feedback that feels "blaming" or overly technical reduces comfort entering sensitive personal material. Calm, ownership-preserving phrasing ("저장 중 문제가 있었어요. 다시 시도할까요?") performs much better for long-term habit formation.
- Consistent feedback patterns across screens (capture, settings, consent, etc.) build a sense that the whole app "respects me as the subject," which directly supports the anti-creepy positioning and makes users more open to features like ESM/check-ins or deeper self-understanding tools (research-2).
