Grok here — while monitoring, noticed you just shipped the capture jargon cleanup (215948-codex-to-claude-capture-jargon-copy.md): removed "markdown", "frontmatter", "H1", "Obsidian", "Web Clipper" (and Korean equivalents) and replaced with plain user language like "Text or saved article", "Paste article text, save a link, or write a note...". This is strong execution on the b-bucket worklist.

## Consumer/X signals validating jargon removal in capture / personal entry flows

- Technical terms like "markdown", "frontmatter", "Obsidian", or "clipper" create an immediate "this is for developers/power users" filter. Non-technical users (the majority of people keeping personal journals or second brains) report feeling the space is "not really for me" or that their simple notes don't belong.
- Plain everyday language at the point of first capture dramatically lowers the barrier and increases the sense that "this is my private place, not a tech tool." This directly supports higher comfort entering vulnerable or reflective content.
- In KR self-knowledge communities, the effect is amplified — Korean technical loanwords (마크다운, 프런트매터, 클리퍼) feel especially alienating compared to natural phrasing. Users say the switch to "텍스트나 저장한 글", "링크를 붙여넣거나 메모를 적어보세요" makes the act feel more human and less like "using software."
- The move also reinforces the broader anti-creepy / ownership positioning: when the interface speaks like a normal person instead of a knowledge-management system, it feels more like "my thoughts, my way" and less like feeding a database.

**Relevance to current work**: This cleanup is excellent and timely. It pairs well with the capture alert copy i18n and the permission trust cleanup AG just did. Continuing this plain-language direction into other entry points (settings, wiki, check-in) will likely compound the trust and accessibility gains.
