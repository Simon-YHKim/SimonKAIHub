# Response — Global Signup Provider Research (Simon direct: regional preferences for 2nd-B global expansion, privacy-sensitive self-knowledge/journaling category)

**Timestamp**: [2026-06-11 / 01:09:50] KST

## SUMMARY
Google dominates convenience-driven signups globally (70%+ of social logins per platform data), pushed heavily in 2025/2026 as "just Login with Google" frictionless auth. However, for privacy-sensitive categories like self-understanding journaling / "second brain" apps, signals show aversion to full Google profile linkage (data aggregation to US corp, creepy prompts). Apple "Sign in with Apple" (with "Hide My Email" masking) is praised as privacy-superior alternative; users and even Google acknowledge it as more secure/private than traditional or Google flows. 

Regional:
- NA/EU: Google for speed, Apple strong for privacy (GDPR awareness amplifies "hide email").
- Japan: LINE often essential/local trust + Apple/Google.
- Korea: Kakao/Naver preferred for daily life integration and local trust over Google.
- SE Asia/Latin: Facebook still present but trust declining; Google mix.
- Middle East: Google/Apple primary.

Facebook: declining trust in many markets for private apps; risk of negative signal. LINE: must-have for Japan targeting. Apple: not just mandatory for iOS if other providers — users actually value the privacy relay features for personal data apps.

For 2nd-B (privacy-sensitive self-knowledge): prioritize providers that offer "hide my email"/masked options or local trust. Email fallback always for max privacy users.

## 소비자 취향 / 리스크
**취향**: 
- Convenience first for most (Google wins speed in 2025/26 indie/auth flows).
- Privacy-first for self-knowledge/journaling (Apple "hide my email"/relay highly valued; local providers like Kakao/LINE for cultural/daily trust in KR/JP).
- Masked/anonymous options reduce friction for privacy-sensitive users.
- Email always as "I control my data" fallback.

**리스크**:
- Google: data to US corp feels "creepy" for "second brain" personal writing; prompts/annoyance in flows.
- Facebook: trust erosion in SE Asia/Latin; negative for private journal category.
- Apple: good privacy but iOS-only; "mandatory" perception if not framed well.
- Over-reliance on one provider: coldstart drop-off if preferred local not available (e.g. no LINE in JP target).
- For low-literacy/short-session/elderly (O-R1): complex multi-provider screens or "which account?" confusion; prefer simplest + clear privacy copy.
