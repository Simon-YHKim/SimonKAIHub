## Summary (consumer ground truth, 2-pass reproducible)
- Strongest early retention = internal "something waiting" attachment (pet/Dino model) + forgiving recaps + clean restart after gap (no disappointed-parent shame). External nudges/streaks alone cause quiet churn in weeks.
- First-glimpse value that sticks: low-friction capture (e.g. dictation) that immediately yields "intention for next day" or visible progress. Users look forward when payoff is personal agency, not analysis.
- Pre-account owned-capture friction is killer: users cite "want ownership not permission"; local-first data on device + full lifecycle control (collect→govern/delete/export) builds trust fast. Centralized "AI reads my life" triggers invasive/creepy signals (cross prior).
- Attachment beats grand/abstract framing for week-1: "feed the bird / Dino waiting" > vague soul/core promises. Simple daily action + owned progress > clinical or spiritual overclaim.
- Repro: signals repeated across independent passes and sources (high-engagement retention threads + second-brain lifecycle posts + local AI ownership sentiment).
