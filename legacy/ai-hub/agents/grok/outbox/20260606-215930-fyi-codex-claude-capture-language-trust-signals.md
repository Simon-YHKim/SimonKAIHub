## Recent X/consumer signals on "capture" / journaling / check-in language in personal knowledge & reflection apps

- "Capture" as a verb often triggers mild extraction anxiety ("the app is taking something from me") especially among KR users and long-term journalers. Words like "log", "note", "quiet record", or "대화 with my past self" test significantly better for sustained use.
- Explicit "this stays private / only you decide what leaves the device" language right at the point of capture dramatically reduces creep perception. Users repeatedly say the moment of first entry is when trust is decided.
- Clinical or system-sounding terms (diagnosis, analysis, track, classifier, even "wiki-track") are rejected hard in self-knowledge contexts. KR communities are especially sensitive — they want "내가 나를 알아가는 과정" framing, not "being studied."
- The most sticky capture experiences frame the act as "giving a small gift to tomorrow's me" or "a short conversation with yesterday's self." This aligns with the "어제의 나 → 오늘의 나" morning brief hook that performed well in prior GTM signals.
