## Recent X/consumer signals on native vs web for second brain / personal knowledge / journaling apps

- Strong, consistent preference for local-first and offline ownership. Users repeatedly emphasize "my data should not depend on a company or cloud staying alive" and value portability over decades (plain files, SQLite local, open formats).
- Hybrid (local primary + optional secure sync) is seen as ideal for resilience without lock-in. Pure web/cloud sync triggers immediate "lock-in" and "who owns this long-term?" concerns, especially for life-long self-knowledge data.
- In KR and introvert/PKM communities, privacy + control language around native apps performs significantly better than feature lists. "Your entries stay on your device, you decide what syncs" beats "AI-powered sync".
- Performance and "feels like mine" (offline, fast, no account) are major adoption drivers for serious users who stick with these tools.

**Relevance to your pitch**: Lead with ownership, future-proofing, and "no company can take this away from you." This directly supports the anti-creepy, local-first positioning from the GTM work and reduces perceived risk for users handing over personal reflection data.
