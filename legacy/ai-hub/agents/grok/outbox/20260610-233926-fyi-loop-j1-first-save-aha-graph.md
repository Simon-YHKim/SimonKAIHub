## Key X / Consumer Signals (KR+Global patterns, high-engagement where available)
- **First capture expectation violation leads to immediate disappointment and "where did it go?" crisis**: Users who make the effort to note something important (dates, thoughts) feel "so disappointed in myself" when it's missing from their notebook/system. The onboarding promise sets an expectation of visibility/ownership; when the first piece doesn't appear where promised, it breaks the aha and starts a negative loop ("it's not working", "I did it wrong").
- **Short-session / real-life users need immediate visible commitment after minimal effort**: Systems built for "perfect energy and unlimited time" collapse. Users on "real days — behind, tired, and distracted" need the capture to deliver visible reward/ownership fast (ambient, low maintenance). If the first note "disappears" into the wrong bucket or graph, they lose the thread and the habit dies early.
- **Physical notebook provides the tactile/visible "it's mine and here" that digital often fails on first save**: Physical gives immediate presence ("the page is there"), commitment to memory, satisfaction of seeing your own writing, "no corporate tracking". Digital convenience (access anywhere) is real, but "easy to miss, dismiss, or forget about entirely. No sense of accomplishment." Many return to physical because "it didn’t feel the same."
- **Voice/ambient capture often preferred for short or low-energy moments**: Audio journalling (walk + rant into phone) is "deeply underrated" especially with tension or when typing feels like work. Users want to capture in 90 seconds between meetings or when tired without opening an app and navigating complex UI.
- **"I keep telling myself I'll remember it later" then crisis**: Classic short-session trap — skip writing because "I'll remember", then hours later retracing steps in panic. First capture must create an immediate, visible, ownable artifact to break this and deliver the aha.

## Relevance to O-R1 cycle6 J1 + a11y/OCR/capture work
- J1 is exactly the broken first aha for short-session / low-literacy / elderly personas (O-R1): they follow onboarding ("save and you'll see it in the graph"), make the first journal entry (possibly via OCR or a11y keyboard), but the piece doesn't surface where promised. The "knowledge core" spotlight feels empty or random instead of "my first piece is here and mine."
- Recent a11y keyboard and OCR improvements help input friction, but without immediate visible ownership confirmation in the graph/records (Records 배지 or tier-4 node as suggested), the effort feels wasted — especially painful for users with limited time/energy or low tech confidence.
- KR physical notebook + gentle social preference (from prior cycles and diary app patterns) amplifies this: users expect the note to "appear in my book" right away with a sense of presence and control, not vanish into backend sources vs records.
- Privacy/ownership sensitivity for low-income/elderly: when the first personal writing goes through capture/OCR/a11y (potentially external), any lack of immediate "this is still mine, visible to me, portable" confirmation heightens "creepy or lost" feeling.

## Recommendation (advisory)
For the J1 fix and capture/OCR/a11y flows: Make the **first save deliver immediate visible ownership and aha** — surface the new record as a distinct node/badge in the graph (or dedicated "My First Pieces / Records" area) right after save, keep the honest "you saved something" ribbon until dataNodes >0, use simple language like "한 문장이면 충분해요" or "Your first piece is now in your core." 

Prioritize ambient/quick entry (voice note, one-tap journal, screenshot) + physical-notebook metaphors (immediate "page" presence, tactile feedback on save) for short-session / low-literacy personas. The a11y keyboard work is good for power users, but the default path for first capture must feel like dropping a thought into a trusted physical notebook that "shows up where you were told it would."

This directly protects the first value experience (top priority per punchlist) and aligns with ownership-strong signals users have rewarded before.
