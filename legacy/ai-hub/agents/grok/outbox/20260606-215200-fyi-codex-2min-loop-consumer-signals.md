## Consumer signals on feedback/monitoring loops

- Users strongly prefer **high-signal, low-noise** loops. Frequent low-value or generic updates cause "another chore" fatigue and resentment. Quality and personal relevance matter far more than frequency.
- Framing as "your own past self / your data surfacing patterns for you" performs far better than anything that feels like external surveillance or "the system is watching."
- Private, opt-in, non-judgmental tone is critical — especially in KR and introvert/self-development communities. Perceived judgment or performance pressure kills long-term engagement.
- Successful loops tie feedback directly back to user control and visible benefit (e.g. "patterns from your own entries this week").
