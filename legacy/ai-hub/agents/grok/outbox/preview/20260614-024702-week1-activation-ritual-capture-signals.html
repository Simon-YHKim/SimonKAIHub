2-Pass Reproducible Signals

  • Habit formation > first download. Daily ritual rare for productivity AI; astrology apps quietly succeed at normalization. Long-term ritual becomes "part of your being" (family/spouse parallel). First-glimpse must deliver immediate emotional payoff to survive day 1. (Q1)
  • Pre-account frictionless capture is high-signal. Users resent aggressive persistent signup nags/banners even when they chose no-account. Expectation: clean UI, journal without login, features reduced until deliberate account, login buried in settings. Direct: "users should be able to journal without login in but the options or features should reduced". (Q2 + acquisition thread)
  • Emotional clarity from raw vent > polished output. Scattered thoughts and inexpressible stress are the norm; users avoid because emotions rarely arrive in perfect sentences. Hook: AI organizes the noise so people understand what they are actually feeling. "Stop holding everything in." Ties to owned-capture: my raw words first. (Q1/Q2)
  • Resonant personal framing spreads. Sentences that surface emergent self ("some parts of you only emerge for certain people") go semi-viral. Journal-to-profile prompts (build identity/thinking style/goals/problems over time from entries) build curiosity. Sharing stronger on authentic self-emergence than generic insight. (Q4)
  • Quiet consistency preferred over gamified pressure. "Stick to one system, journal it, review it, give time." Micro-pause-and-choose lighter than forced daily ritual. Presence increases when user feels control of capture (human summary > AI because "I’m chill and focused"). (Q1/Q4)
  • Competitor perception wedge. Account-required + naggy onboarding surfaces as friction. Clean local/no-account options praised when truly clean. Ownership and first-use simplicity are live differentiators in complaints vs Notion AI / Day One category. (Q5 + 경쟁 thread)
