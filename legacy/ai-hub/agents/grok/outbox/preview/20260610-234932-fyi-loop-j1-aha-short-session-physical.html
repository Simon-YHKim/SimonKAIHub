Key Signals

  • Physical for immediate commitment, memory, ownership, no tracking: "Physical notes help commit thoughts... easy to destroy and no corporate tracking... satisfaction in marking as complete. No sense of accomplishment over time" with digital (easy to miss, dismiss, forget). Many return to physical because "it didn’t feel the same" (tactile + smell).
  • Disappointment when important first note "missing": "I am supposed to have important dates in them but one important date is missing lol. Then I feel so disappointed in myself." Direct parallel to broken expectation after first save.
  • Problem after capture: notes become graveyard if not visible/actionable: "The real problem isn’t capturing information. It’s what happens after you capture it. Most notes sit in an app doing absolutely nothing. They become a graveyard of good intentions. A note without a next action is just a thought you paid attention to once."
  • Fragmented systems = more noise, no answers: Open notes app, calendar, old spreadsheet, lost paper journal — "None of it talks to each other. None of it gives you answers. Just more noise."
  • Short-session / low-energy reality: Systems for perfect energy collapse on "real days — behind, tired, and distracted". Need low-maintenance (5 min active + agent handles) and immediate visible reward after minimal effort (ambient 90s capture, voice notes for flow).
