# Outside View (밖에서 본 나) Safe Framing Validation — Consumer Research Pointer

**Summary (X/social + reviews + consumer signals)**: Validated three specific framing/rollout questions for 2nd-B planned "Outside view" / peer-eval layer. Full findings delivered as self-contained HTML report per PROTOCOL §10.6.

## Validated Points (exec)
1. **Mirror / blind-spot / 360-for-self-understanding / Johari positioning lands better** than "peer evaluation/rating". Existing consumer tools (johari-window.com, SelfStir "friends context") use exactly this language and position as self-dev/safe. "Rating/eval" triggers immediate creepiness (Peeple precedent) + high opt-out/stress in 360 data (79% would opt out, 35% stress). X/consumer discourse around Johari is educational + growth-positive; direct "rate me" rarely surfaces positively in personal social graphs.

2. **Positive+strengths-only at launch, then optionally balanced**: Strong consumer appetite for low-anxiety entry (tbh/Gas viral constrained-positive pattern; Johari adjective positive lists used in personal/team contexts). Rater willingness higher (easier to give nice/structured), receiver dread lower. Risks: politeness/sugarcoating bias (reduces blind-spot accuracy), "fluffy/not real" perception. Transition to balanced viable after trust built, if "growth suggestions / observed impact" not "criticism/weaknesses", aggregate-first, full recipient control + opt-out. Workplace 360 shows balanced always carries relationship-strain risk; personal trusted-circle amplifies need for phased safe start.

3. **Optimal invite-message copy for safe adoption without creepiness**: 
   - Core: "self-understanding / personal growth / mirror exercise", "trusted few", "optional no pressure", "results private to me", "strengths or one helpful observation".
   - Avoids judgment-anxiety triggers: "rate/evaluate/judge me", "be honest about flaws", "what do you really think", public/leaderboard hints, always-on, open free-text.
   - Tested patterns from live tools + psych best-practice: shareable link + 2-3 min structured + explicit "only if feels good".
   - Full recommended copy variants + rationale in HTML.
