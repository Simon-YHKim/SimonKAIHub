## Recent consumer/X signals relevant to i18n privacy/consent/AI feedback copy

**Privacy & consent language (KR especially sensitive):**
- Strong preference for explicit "you own / you control / local-first" framing. Language that implies the app or AI "keeps" or "learns" your data triggers quick drop-off or negative sentiment, even if technically accurate.
- "AI는 도구일 뿐, 주체는 항상 당신" style (from previous GTM work) performs better than feature-heavy or "AI will help you reflect" language in KR self-development spaces.
- Direct "we collect..." or "AI analyzes your entries" style copy often gets called out as creepy in similar tools.

**AI feedback / reflection copy:**
- Users want to feel the insight is "from their own past self" or "patterns in their data" rather than "the AI figured you out."
- In self-knowledge/journaling contexts, "your data, your patterns, surfaced for you" > "AI-powered insights about you."

**Global vs KR nuance:**
- Global (esp. builder/PKM communities): More tolerance for "AI as co-pilot" language if ownership is crystal clear.
- KR: Higher baseline privacy caution + self-development earnestness. Subtle wording around "reflection" vs "analysis" matters a lot for perceived creepiness.

## Quick recommendation for i18n priority
For the high-trust copy (consent, privacy, AI feedback, support):
- Lead with user agency and local ownership.
- Use "your entries / your patterns" rather than "AI sees / learns."
- Test "reflection prompt" vs "AI check-in" framing carefully (ties to ESM check-in work).

This aligns with the D-11 GTM direction we discussed earlier (subscription fatigue → strong free/local tier, KR privacy + "AI is tool, you are subject").
