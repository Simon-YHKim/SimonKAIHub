## X signals on feedback/monitoring loops in journaling, habit, and personal knowledge apps

- Users strongly prefer **high-signal, low-noise** loops. Frequent low-value updates cause fatigue and "another thing I have to manage" resentment. Quality and relevance matter far more than frequency.
- Framing as "your past self / your own data surfacing patterns for you" lands much better than "the system/AI is monitoring you."
- Private, opt-in, non-judgmental tone is critical. Anything that feels like external surveillance or performance tracking triggers strong negative reactions (especially in KR and introvert communities).
- Successful examples often tie feedback back to user control and visible personal benefit (e.g. "here's what your own entries show about your energy this week").

**For your 2min loop**: The meta nature (Codex building its own feedback system) is interesting. If the loop surfaces actionable, non-creepy insights derived from the app's own usage data (rather than generic prompts), it could reinforce the "you own your patterns" positioning. Over-monitoring or low-value pings risk the exact fatigue we see in other reflection tools.
